Sea View Star
Short practice passage

Sylvie and Sammy’s teacher has announced that students will do a project for “Make a Difference Day.” This means students will find a special way to help others. Sylvie and Sammy are doing the project together.

Sylvie tapped Sammy’s shoulder and pointed to the swing set. They always got their best ideas on the swings. “Race you up!” She hopped on a swing and pumped her legs. Sammy followed.

“We need to do something extra stellar for the animals,” said Sylvie.

Sammy had ideas too. When they looked at their lists, they discovered they both wanted to hold an art show to raise money for animals.

“Ready to start planning our art show for animals?” asked Sylvie. “Or ‘Paint Out!’ Sounds more stellar, doesn’t it?”

They decided people could paint pictures of their pets outside the shops on Beach Street. Then they could sell the paintings and donate the money to the Sea View Animal Shelter.

Before dinner, they had already gotten permission from the town, informed the animal shelter, and told friends and family to save the date. When Make a Difference Day arrived, everything was ready.

By the end, the Paint Out was full of people, pets, colors, and excitement. Sylvie looked around proudly as the event became a huge success.
